Ponder and Prove

Your Tasks

We are learning about compression algorithms this week.

The life lesson is to learn to eliminate waste — compress the redundancy (uselessness) out of your life.

Sin is waste. It is doing one thing when you should be doing other and better things for which you have the capacity. Hence, there are no innocent idle thoughts. That is why even the righteous must repent, constantly and progressively, since all fall short of their capacity and calling. — Hugh Nibley, Zeal Without Knowledge

Find and read the whole essay quoted from above. Be prepared to discuss it.

Figure out where you waste your life or your resources, and begin to formulate a plan, even an algorithm, to eliminate that waste.

Talk to your acquaintances who have followed prophetic counsel to store food and other essentials.

Investigate how much food storage gets wasted. How and why is there so much waste?

How to Fulfill Return-and-Report Requirements

  • Make sure you take notes on the conversations you have outside of class
  • Prepare to share with your classroom team your discussion notes and discoveries about the algorithms you found that you use to bring order to your life and make it better.
