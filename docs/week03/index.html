Algorithms for Ordered Searching

The life lesson this week is on the importance of and need for preparation, or what Dr. Stephen R. Covey, amoung others, called saw-sharpening. The analagy is this. If you sharpen your saw before you start using it to cut wood, it is faster and easier to complete your task.

Imagine that your cloths are piled up in the middle of your bedroom and you need to get dressed to come to class. Also imagine that you just threw your cloths in the pile with absolutely no thought. There is more thing regarding this scenario. Your roommate, or spouse, is not happy with the situation and has stirred up your pile in a pique.

Now I'm sure none of you have ever piled your cloths up like this.😄 But if you were in this situation, how efficienctly and quickly could you get dressed?

Or maybe, like me, you grew up in a home where there was a junk drawer. A drawer where you put everything that didn't seem to fit into the groups of things in the other drawers. How easy was it to find something in there?

If you are looking for something specific in a group of unordered things, your search is going to be frustrating and slow. There is a better way.

Ordered Search

Last week you learned at least one algorithm for sorting things. It is much more efficient to search through an ordered set of things when you are looking for something specific. So, before searching you should sort the things. Then you can find what you need easier.

Binary search is a common algorithm used to search ordered things. In binary search you start by looking in the middle. I know. That's weird. But stop and think about it for a minute. If you start in the middle, then you know if the thing you are looking for is to your right or your left. That means you don't have to check HALF of the things to find what you are looking for. If you now ignore the half of the things that can't be what you want, you can repeat this process for the half where the thing you want might be until you find it. That's going to be a lot faster than starting on the left or right and checking each thing until you find what you want.🎉🎉

Binary search can be hard to imagine so you might want to watch a visualization of binary search.
