CSE 180: Week 04

Ponder and Prove

Your Tasks

This week's life lesson is:

Be efficient and effective by finding a good path to your goals in a vert beneficial way.

Your first task is to evaluate your path to graduation. Your “grad plan” is meant to help you reach your goal of graduating with the degree you want. In terms of efficiency and effectiveness, how good is your current grad plan? While the university requires you to use the “grad planner” tool, YOU should use it to think about and explore the many paths to graduation starting from where you are right now. Those paths are part of the graph that is all classes and majors at BYUI. Explore what the grad planner tool can do for you when you keep efficiency in mind. Also realize there are many other people and resources you have at your disposal to help you explore and to graduate. Find them. They may be professors, councilors, bishops, friends, advisors, or any other employee of student of the university.

Your second task is to find other times and/or places where you and your acquaintances are using graphs and path-finding to improve your lives.

How to Fulfill Return-and-Report Requirements

  • Make sure you take notes on your grad planner explorations and the conversations you have outside of class.
  • Prepare to share with your classroom team your discussion notes and discoveries about the algorithms you investigated.
