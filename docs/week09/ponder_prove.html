CSE 180: Week 09

Ponder and Prove

Your Tasks

This week you will begin to explore how to use calculation to assess and classify how sorting before applying an algorithm makes search algorithms more efficient, and by how much.

“Avoidance strategies” are all about avoiding work, or learning to do it faster and better. For example, learning from washing each dish how to wash the next one faster.

Find some examples of avoidance strategies. (You could google this.) Then ask someone to sort their junk drawer. Observe their process. Is it random or deliberate and systematic?

Did they learn anything other than that they have a lot of junk?

Help them see that having sorted their junk, the next time they put something in that drawer it will be far easier to keep it organized.

Also figure out the O-complexity of the algorithms you collected during week 3.

How to Fulfill Return-and-Report Requirements

  • Make sure you take notes and write a report for your teammates on the conversations you have outside of class. Here are some ideas you can use to improve the quality of the report.

    • What are your relationships to the people you interviewed?
    • What are the detailed, discrete, algorithm steps they used?
    • Why did they choose to do it that way?
    • What are your ideas for how they could improve this algorithm?
    Do not limit yourself to these questions.

  • Prepare to share with your classroom team your report and discoveries about the algorithms you investigated.
