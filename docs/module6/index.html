CSE 180: Module 6

Reducing Waste

Use the resources of the earth sparingly and reverently. Adopt lifestyles and personal habits that respect the Creation. As you can, fix up and keep clean the places where you live, work, recreate, and worship.

- The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints

Please read all of the linked articles.

This week, the life lesson is to learn about how to eliminate waste in our lives. This includes compressing the redundancy (uselessness) out of our lives and focusing on replacing good things with better and best things.

Waste

Waste is a big part of most of our lives. Many of us live in places where there is such an abundance that we have become used to waste and no longer see it. Consider how much food is wasted by grocery stores in the United States each day when they throw out food that has become wilted, old, or rotten and replace it with new food, that they know will not be sold before it too goes bad, and do this for the sake of marketing not due to poor planning.

Grocery stores are not the only food wasters. In an article from the BYU paper, they quote the US Department of Agriculture as stating that 30-40% of all food in the US ends up as waste. This happens all the way from production to individuals throwing out food from their refrigerators and plates.

The church has produced a Gospel Topics article on environmental stewardship and conservation. In it each of us is taught that we have a responsibility to keep the earth clean. That includes being aware of how the trash, food and non-food waste, we generate is dealt with.

But wasting resources is not the only type of waste we see around us or even that we participate in. As human beings we waste our time doing good things when we could be doing better or best things. In 2007 Elder Oaks taught us in his conference talk called Good, Better, Best to carefully think about the things we choose to do. There are good things we do each day that could be replaced by things that are better or best.

Earlier, Hugh Nibley put it this way.

Sin is waste. It is doing one thing when you should be doing other and better things for which you have the capacity. Hence, there are no innocent idle thoughts. That is why even the righteous must repent, constantly and progressively, since all fall short of their capacity and calling.

Hugh Nibley - " Zeal Without Knowledge", 1978
